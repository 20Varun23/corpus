WHAT IS IMPULSE?

Impulse is a first of its kind, 48-hour signal processing hackathon conducted by IEEE NITK student branch in association with IEEE NITK SPS student chapter. This edition Impulse is being organized in collaboration with TIMED AIIMS Delhi. It presents you with interesting problem statements to ensure a fun-filled learning experience. In addition to the hackathon, expert talks will also be conducted prior to the final event to enhance your experience with us! Lunch shall also be provied on 18th January

Open to B.Tech and MBBS students all over India


Speaker

Building Trustworthy Deep Learning Tools with EEG to Understand Neurological Disorders

By Deeksha Shama Deeksha M Shama is a PhD student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Johns Hopkins University and a Visiting Researcher at Boston University, working under the guidance of Prof. Archana Venkataraman. She completed her undergraduate degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from NITK. Her research spans the intersection of probabilistic machine learning, deep learning, signal processing, and medical imaging, with a focus on high-dimensional signal analysis. She is particularly interested in developing interpretable AI models for clinical use, addressing challenges like small datasets and noisy EEG data in populations with neurological disorders such as epilepsy and autism.
